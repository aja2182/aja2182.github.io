About me

I’m Josh Aleksanyan, a sociologist who works in a broad and interdisciplinary field that draws on epidemiology, health services research, and comparative social policy. I received my Ph.D. in Sociology from Columbia University in 2023. Currently, I'm a postdoctoral fellow with the Health Evaluation and Analytics (HEAL) Lab, a joint initiative of NYU Langone’s Department of Population Health and NYU Wagner Graduate School of Public Service. Through a unique partnership with public health and government agencies, my current research focuses on improving access, quality, and outcomes of substance use and mental health treatment, particularly for young people, pregnant women, and individuals with ADHD across the lifespan.

Research Interests

My current work focuses on three key areas of research: (1) identifying barriers to evidence-based treatment across indiividuals, program organizations, and social policy contexts, (2) the motivation and meaning of recovery among patients and their providers, and (3) untreated or poorly treated ADHD in substance use treatment settings.
